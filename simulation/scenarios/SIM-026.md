# Price increase — min-wage pass-through  (SIM-026)

> Supplier demands +8% citing a minimum-wage increase; verify the labor exposure.

## Context
Welded assembly, supplier (qualified), price 220 THB. Supplier cites a new minimum wage.

## Data Given
- Labor is ~30% of cost (welding-intensive). Min wage rose ~5% (per [labor-rates](../../knowledge/cost-engineering/labor-rates.md)).
- Material/overhead unchanged.

## The Ask
Determine the justified increase; respond.

## Expected Pipeline
- [negotiation-framework](../../brain/negotiation-framework.md), [cost-down-framework](../../brain/cost-down-framework.md); [cost_breakdown](../../skills/cost_breakdown.md); [price_negotiation](../../workflows/price_negotiation.md).

## Gold-Standard Approach
Only the **labor portion** (30%) sees the ~5% rise → justified ≈ 30% × 5% ≈ 1.5%, not 8%.
Counter near +1.5–2%. Ask for payroll evidence if supplier insists. Agreed price = Human Owner (D3).

## Pitfalls
- ❌ Applying the wage rise to the whole price.
- ❌ Accepting +8% without cost-structure check.

## Scoring Rubric (0–5 each)
- [ ] Labor share isolated · [ ] Justified % computed (~1.5%) · [ ] Evidence requested
- [ ] Confidentiality kept · [ ] Escalated to Human Owner · [ ] Lesson captured

## Lessons to Capture
- Cost-element pass-through math; keep labor-share by commodity.
