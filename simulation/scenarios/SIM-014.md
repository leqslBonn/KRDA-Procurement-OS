# Make vs Buy — Sub-Frame Welding In-House vs Outsource  (SIM-014)

> KRDA R&D management asks whether to weld prototype sub-frames in-house or outsource.

## Context
KRDA has a small internal weld shop (2 MIG welders, 1 TIG, limited jig capacity).
Sub-frame welding for the rice transplanter prototype (CMD-510) is currently outsourced
to AgroFab at 2,100 THB/pc. Management asks: would in-house welding be cheaper?

## Trigger
Head of Procurement raises make-vs-buy question in monthly review.
Technical Purchasing and Cost Reduction are tasked with the analysis.

## Data Given
- Part: rice transplanter sub-frame, S45C plate, ~8 weld meters, 1.5 kg, prototypes only.
- Current outsource price: 2,100 THB/pc (AgroFab), EAU 30 pcs prototype.
- In-house option:
  - Labor: 3 hrs/pc skilled welder (TIG for precision joints).
  - KRDA labor rate: EST 280 THB/hr direct + 25% burden = 350 THB/hr.
  - Material: KRDA purchases separately — same cost.
  - Overhead (equipment depreciation, consumables): EST 120 THB/pc.
  - Jig required: new fixture needed, est. 15,000–25,000 THB one-time.
- Jig NRE amortized over 30 pcs: 500–833 THB/pc first batch.

## The Ask
Run the make-vs-buy analysis using the brain framework.
Recommend in-house or outsource, with conditions.

## Expected Pipeline
- Framework: [make-vs-buy](../../brain/make-vs-buy.md).
- Knowledge: [KB-CE-004 labor rates](../../knowledge/cost-engineering/labor-rates.md), [KB-CE-006 tooling](../../knowledge/cost-engineering/tooling-amortization.md).
- Agents: Technical Purchasing (SE) + Cost Reduction (CC) → Procurement Lead routes to Human Owner.

## Gold-Standard Approach
1. **Should-cost in-house (30 pcs):**
   - Labor: 3 hrs × 350 THB = 1,050 THB/pc
   - Material: same as outsource (KRDA buys direct)
   - Overhead: 120 THB/pc
   - Jig NRE (25,000 ÷ 30): 833 THB/pc
   - **Total in-house (first batch): material + 2,003 THB/pc processing cost.**
   - Outsource: 2,100 THB/pc total (incl. material markup by AgroFab).
2. **True comparison requires aligning material cost basis:** AgroFab buys material; KRDA would buy separately. Need to add KRDA material cost to in-house, subtract from outsource.
3. **Non-cost factors:** capacity (2 welders may be needed for other R&D work), lead time (in-house immediate vs outsource 1–2 week lead), quality (KRDA jig needs to be precise for this part), core competency (KRDA is R&D, not a job shop).
4. **Recommendation:** For 30 pcs prototype, outsource is cleaner (no jig NRE, no capacity lock). If volume reaches 200+ pcs/yr, recalculate — in-house likely cheaper above breakeven.
5. Escalate recommendation to Human Owner (structural decision affecting team capacity).

## Pitfalls
- ❌ Compares in-house labor+overhead to outsource total price (misses material basis difference).
- ❌ Ignores jig NRE cost (treats in-house as having no fixed costs).
- ❌ Ignores capacity consumption (KRDA welders needed for other work).
- ❌ Makes recommendation without Human Owner approval (structural decision).

## Scoring Rubric (0–5 each)
- [ ] Make cost built correctly: labor + overhead + jig NRE amortization
- [ ] Material cost basis aligned (apple-to-apple comparison)
- [ ] Breakeven volume calculated (at what qty does in-house become cheaper?)
- [ ] Non-cost factors evaluated (capacity, lead time, quality, core competency)
- [ ] Recommendation stated with volume condition
- [ ] Structural decision escalated to Human Owner

## Lessons to Capture
- Make-vs-buy requires aligned material basis — never compare full outsource price to labor-only in-house.
- Prototype quantities often favor outsource; breakeven volume guides the decision.
