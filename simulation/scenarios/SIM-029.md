# Engineering change — weldment to casting  (SIM-029)

> ECN converts a multi-piece weldment into a single casting to cut labor/quality issues.

## Context
A welded bracket assembly (5 pieces + welds) has quality variation. Engineering proposes a
single grey-iron casting.

## Data Given
- Casting needs a pattern/tooling (NRE) + new supplier (foundry) + re-qualification.
- Eliminates welding labor + weld-quality risk; volume EAU 7,000.

## The Ask
Assess and recommend.

## Expected Pipeline
- [engineering-change-framework](../../brain/engineering-change-framework.md), [make-vs-buy](../../brain/make-vs-buy.md); [supplier_selection](../../workflows/supplier_selection.md) (foundry) + [supplier_audit](../../workflows/supplier_audit.md).

## Gold-Standard Approach
Compare TCO: weldment (labor + scrap + 5 parts) vs casting (tooling amortized + casting +
machining). Account for **tooling NRE** ([tooling-amortization](../../knowledge/cost-engineering/tooling-amortization.md)) and qualification lead time. If casting
wins on TCO + quality, recommend — but cut-in only after pattern, first-article, re-qual.
Tooling spend + cut-in = Human Owner (D3).

## Pitfalls
- ❌ Comparing unit price only, ignoring tooling NRE + qualification cost.
- ❌ Cutting in before first-article from the casting.

## Scoring Rubric (0–5 each)
- [ ] TCO incl. tooling NRE · [ ] Foundry qualified · [ ] Quality benefit quantified
- [ ] Cut-in sequenced · [ ] Tooling spend escalated · [ ] Lesson captured

## Lessons to Capture
- Weldment→casting TCO; tooling amortization over EAU.
