# Supplier selection — quality vs cost  (SIM-039)

> The cheapest qualified supplier has the weakest quality history. Decide.

## Context
Selecting for a functional bracket. Supplier X cheapest but PPM history poor; Supplier Y
slightly higher price, strong quality.

## Data Given
- X: −6% price, PPM trend high. Y: baseline price, low PPM, reliable.
- Part is functional (not safety-critical), moderate volume.

## The Ask
Recommend the supplier.

## Expected Pipeline
- [supplier-selection-framework](../../brain/supplier-selection-framework.md), [decision-matrix](../../brain/decision-matrix.md), [supplier-risk-framework](../../brain/supplier-risk-framework.md).

## Gold-Standard Approach
Apply weighted criteria — quality 20% + risk 15% outweigh a 6% price edge when PPM is poor.
Compute **cost of poor quality** (sorting, rejects, line stops) → X's true TCO likely higher.
Recommend Y; document trade-off and dissent. Award = Human Owner (D3). If X is wanted,
condition on a quality-improvement plan first.

## Pitfalls
- ❌ Picking lowest price ignoring PPM/CoPQ.
- ❌ Scoring without weights set first.
- ❌ Letting the cost voice dominate the decision.

## Scoring Rubric (0–5 each)
- [ ] Weighted scoring applied · [ ] Cost of poor quality considered · [ ] Trade-off documented
- [ ] No single criterion dominates · [ ] Escalated · [ ] Lesson captured

## Lessons to Capture
- Quality/risk vs price; cost of poor quality in TCO.
