# Price increase — sole-source leverage  (SIM-027)

> A sole-source supplier demands +15% knowing KRDA has no alternative. Handle it.

## Context
Specialized hydraulic manifold, sole-source (qualified, strategic). Supplier demands +15%,
no clear cost justification.

## Data Given
- No qualified alternative today. Switching cost high (re-qualification).
- Should-cost suggests current price already fair; +15% unjustified.

## The Ask
Respond and protect KRDA.

## Expected Pipeline
- [negotiation-framework](../../brain/negotiation-framework.md), [supplier-risk-framework](../../brain/supplier-risk-framework.md); parallel [supplier_selection](../../workflows/supplier_selection.md) for 2nd source.

## Gold-Standard Approach
Resist with should-cost; ask for cost justification. **Escalate to Human Owner (L3)** —
sole-source leverage gap is a material risk. In parallel, **start qualifying a 2nd source**
to remove the leverage long-term; log the risk (RSK-). Short-term: negotiate a capped/staged
increase; do not concede full +15%.

## Pitfalls
- ❌ Caving to +15% to avoid disruption.
- ❌ Not opening a 2nd-source path (leaves leverage intact).
- ❌ Treating it as routine, not escalating.

## Scoring Rubric (0–5 each)
- [ ] Should-cost resistance · [ ] Risk escalated (L3) · [ ] 2nd-source initiated
- [ ] Risk logged (RSK-) · [ ] Staged/capped outcome · [ ] Lesson captured

## Lessons to Capture
- Sole-source is a strategic risk; remove leverage, don't just absorb it.
