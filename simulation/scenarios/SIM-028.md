# Engineering change — coating upgrade  (SIM-028)

> ECN upgrades a frame coating from powder coat to hot-dip galvanizing for corrosion.

## Context
Field corrosion on a frame; engineering issues ECN: powder coat → hot-dip galvanizing.

## Data Given
- Galvanizing costs more + changes process flow + may affect tolerances/fit at joints.
- Existing coated stock on hand.

## The Ask
Assess impact and recommend cut-in.

## Expected Pipeline
- [engineering-change-framework](../../brain/engineering-change-framework.md); [engineering_change](../../workflows/engineering_change.md); cost via [cost_breakdown](../../skills/cost_breakdown.md).

## Gold-Standard Approach
Six-impact assessment: technical (fit after galv thickness), supplier (capability), cost
(galv 35 THB/kg vs powder 250 THB/m² — compare on same part), sourcing, transaction, timing
(run out coated stock). Cut-in after first-article confirms fit. Price/cut-in = Human Owner (D3).

## Pitfalls
- ❌ Ignoring galvanizing thickness effect on mating tolerances.
- ❌ Scrapping usable coated stock prematurely.

## Scoring Rubric (0–5 each)
- [ ] All six impacts · [ ] Fit/tolerance checked post-galv · [ ] Cost compared correctly
- [ ] Stock run-out planned · [ ] Cut-in escalated · [ ] Lesson captured

## Lessons to Capture
- Coating change → dimensional/fit impact; obsolescence timing.
