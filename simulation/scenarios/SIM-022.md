# RFQ — wiring harness  (SIM-022)

> Quote a low-complexity electrical harness; speed and lead time matter.

## Context
Wiring harness for harvester control box. EAU 4,000. Commodity-ish; 3 harness makers.

## Data Given
- Should-cost ≈ 180 THB (connectors + wire + assembly labor).
- Quotes (synthetic): A 175, B 190, C 170 — all qualified.

## The Ask
Award the harness.

## Expected Pipeline
- [rfq](../../workflows/rfq.md); [compare_quotation](../../skills/compare_quotation.md); [rfq-evaluation](../../brain/rfq-evaluation-framework.md).

## Gold-Standard Approach
All qualified → compare on normalized total value + lead time + RoHS compliance.
C lowest and qualified → recommend C; confirm lead time and compliance docs. Straightforward
D3 award. Keep ≥3 quotes.

## Pitfalls
- ❌ Ignoring RoHS/compliance evidence on electronics.
- ❌ Over-engineering a simple commodity decision.

## Scoring Rubric (0–5 each)
- [ ] Normalized compare · [ ] Compliance checked · [ ] ≥3 quotes
- [ ] Lead time considered · [ ] Award escalated · [ ] Lesson captured

## Lessons to Capture
- Electronics compliance (RoHS/REACH) as an evaluation gate.
