# Cost down — bearing standardization  (SIM-024)

> Multiple harvester models use near-identical bearings; consolidate for volume.

## Context
4 models use 5 similar bearing sizes; fragmented buying, low volume per part.

## Data Given
- Combined annual volume high; per-part volume low → no scale leverage.
- Engineering says 5 sizes could reduce to 2 with minor design alignment.

## The Ask
Recommend a standardization/consolidation saving.

## Expected Pipeline
- [cost-down-framework](../../brain/cost-down-framework.md) (volume/consolidation lever); cross-model design via [engineering_change](../../workflows/engineering_change.md).

## Gold-Standard Approach
Map the 5 → 2; quantify volume leverage + inventory reduction. Needs engineering buy-in
(change framework) for the alignment. Negotiate the consolidated volume; validate saving.
Multi-part change + price = Human Owner (D3). Reduces SKUs + supply risk too.

## Pitfalls
- ❌ Forcing standardization that compromises a model's function.
- ❌ Counting saving before engineering confirms interchangeability.

## Scoring Rubric (0–5 each)
- [ ] Consolidation mapped · [ ] Volume + inventory benefit quantified · [ ] Engineering aligned
- [ ] Saving validated · [ ] Escalated · [ ] Lesson captured

## Lessons to Capture
- SKU rationalization as a cost + risk lever.
