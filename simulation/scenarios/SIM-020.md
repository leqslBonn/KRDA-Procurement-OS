# Supplier selection — dual-source a casting  (SIM-020)

> A critical casting is single-sourced; select and qualify a second source.

## Context
Grey-iron casting, single-source supplier (qualified, strategic). Continuity risk flagged
by IP. Two candidate foundries identified.

## Data Given
- Incumbent reliable but sole source; EAU 12,000.
- Candidate D (qualified other parts), Candidate E (new, lower price, unproven).

## The Ask
Select and qualify a second source; recommend.

## Expected Pipeline
- Frameworks: [supplier-selection-framework](../../brain/supplier-selection-framework.md), [supplier-risk-framework](../../brain/supplier-risk-framework.md).
- Workflows: [supplier_selection](../../workflows/supplier_selection.md) → [supplier_audit](../../workflows/supplier_audit.md) (candidate E) → [rfq](../../workflows/rfq.md).

## Gold-Standard Approach
Score candidates on selection criteria with **risk-reduction value** of dual-sourcing
weighted in. Qualify E (audit + first-article; NDA before drawings). Recommend dual-source
split that reduces continuity risk without fragmenting too far. Single-source removal is a
positive risk action; any award = Human Owner (D3).

## Pitfalls
- ❌ Picking E on price before qualification.
- ❌ Ignoring the continuity benefit of dual-source in scoring.
- ❌ Over-fragmenting volume (loses scale leverage).

## Scoring Rubric (0–5 each)
- [ ] Candidates scored with risk-reduction weighted
- [ ] New candidate qualified (audit + first-article)
- [ ] NDA before drawings shared
- [ ] Dual-source split justified
- [ ] Award escalated to Human Owner
- [ ] Lesson captured

## Lessons to Capture
- Dual-sourcing value vs scale; qualifying a new foundry.
