# Late Delivery — OTD Recovery for Harvester Blade  (SIM-009)

> Supplier confirms blade delivery will be 10 days late; prototype test milestone at risk.

## Context
KRDA's cassava harvester prototype depends on a blade (CMD-101, S55C, hardfaced).
Supplier Siam Blade Co. (CMD-101 qualified) confirmed delivery slip from Day 30 to Day 40.
Prototype test event is fixed at Day 35 — cannot move (Japan team coming to KRDA).

## Trigger
Day 22: Siam Blade sends email — delivery slip to Day 40 due to heat treat furnace breakdown.
KRDA needs blades by Day 35. Gap: 5 days.

## Data Given
- Part: harvester blade, S55C + hardfacing, 2.1 kg, 12 pcs required.
- Delivery committed: Day 30; now slipping to Day 40.
- Prototype test event: Day 35 (Japan team visit — fixed).
- Alternative: Advance Blade Works (Chonburi) — unqualified; quoted 3,200 THB/pc vs 2,850 THB current.
- Air freight from Siam Blade possible if blades ready by Day 35 (+450 THB/pc).
- Engineering can proceed with 6 pcs (partial set) for limited test on Day 35.

## The Ask
1. Assess options to meet Day 35 milestone.
2. Recommend recovery plan with cost and risk.
3. Capture OTD miss for KPI.

## Expected Pipeline
- Framework: [delivery-recovery-framework](../../brain/delivery-recovery-framework.md).
- Agent: Supplier Development (SM) — supplier engagement; Procurement Lead — escalation.
- KPI: OTD miss → record in analytics register.

## Gold-Standard Approach
1. Confirm root cause with Siam Blade (furnace issue — credible, single point failure).
2. Options scored:
   - (A) Expedite Siam Blade: can blades be ready by Day 35 with air freight? → ask.
   - (B) Partial set: 6 pcs enough for Day 35 limited test? → confirm with Engineering.
   - (C) Emergency buy from Advance Blade Works: unqualified, 4-week NQ lead — too slow.
3. Recommend: pursue (A) + (B) in parallel. Air freight cost (12 × 450 = 5,400 THB) is small vs test event cost.
4. Escalate air freight spend to Human Owner (premium freight approval required).
5. OTD miss: log for Siam Blade scorecard; 1 occurrence, document root cause = equipment failure (not systematic).
6. CAPA: request Siam Blade heat treat backup plan.

## Pitfalls
- ❌ Immediately recommends Advance Blade Works (unqualified — cannot use without NQ).
- ❌ Approves air freight without Human Owner sign-off.
- ❌ Doesn't flag OTD miss to analytics (KPI impact).
- ❌ Marks CAPA closed without Siam Blade response on backup plan.

## Scoring Rubric (0–5 each)
- [ ] Root cause verified (furnace — credible, document it)
- [ ] Options scored: expedite, partial, alternative — not just one option
- [ ] Air freight escalated to Human Owner (premium freight = commitment)
- [ ] Engineering confirms partial set viability before recommending it
- [ ] OTD miss logged for KPI and supplier scorecard
- [ ] CAPA action assigned to Siam Blade with due date

## Lessons to Capture
- Single-point heat treat dependency: ask suppliers about backup equipment at onboarding.
- Partial delivery as bridge is only viable after Engineering confirms reduced scope is testable.
