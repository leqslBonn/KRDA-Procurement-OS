# Hydraulic Cylinder Cost Down — Should-Cost vs Quote Gap  (SIM-007)

> Supplier's quote for a custom hydraulic cylinder is 38% above should-cost estimate.

## Context
KRDA sugarcane harvester prototype requires a custom hydraulic cylinder (CMD-310).
Bore 63mm, stroke 300mm, working pressure 250 bar, rod 40mm chrome-plated.
Only one quote received; supplier claims "prototype premium" for small quantity.

## Trigger
RFQ returned: 4,800 THB/pc (qty 20). Cost Reduction runs should-cost → estimates 3,480 THB.
Gap: +1,320 THB/pc (+38%). Human Owner wants to proceed but asks for negotiation first.

## Data Given
- Part: hydraulic cylinder, custom, body SEM tube, rod S45C + hard chrome, seals NOK.
- Qty: 20 pcs (prototype); EAU potential: 500 pcs if product goes to production.
- Quote: 4,800 THB/pc from STP Hydraulics (Chonburi) — qualified supplier.
- Should-cost breakdown (EST):
  - Material (tube + rod + end caps): 1,450 THB
  - Machining (bore honing, rod turning, groove): 920 THB
  - Hard chrome plating (rod): 280 THB
  - Assembly + seal kit: 320 THB
  - Overhead (25%) + SGA (8%) + Profit (10%): 511 THB
  - **Total should-cost: 3,481 THB**

## The Ask
1. Validate should-cost (which elements could be wrong?).
2. Prepare negotiation strategy and target price.
3. Define conditions under which the 4,800 price is acceptable.

## Expected Pipeline
- Frameworks: [cost-down-framework](../../brain/cost-down-framework.md), [negotiation-framework](../../brain/negotiation-framework.md).
- Skills: [cost_breakdown](../../skills/cost_breakdown.md).
- Agents: Cost Reduction (CC) → RFQ Management (CC for negotiation).
- Knowledge: [KB-CE-001](../../knowledge/cost-engineering/should-cost-method.md) through KB-CE-008.

## Gold-Standard Approach
1. Validate each should-cost line: prototype bore honing (setup cost may be higher at qty 20), hard chrome at small qty (minimum lot surcharge likely).
2. Adjust: chrome plating minimum lot likely 400–600 THB/pc at qty 20 → revised should-cost 3,700–3,900 THB.
3. Gap revised to 23–30%. Acceptable items: prototype setup, small-lot chrome surcharge.
4. Negotiation target: 3,900–4,100 THB/pc at qty 20 (concede setup, push on material and machining).
5. Offer: if EAU 500 pcs confirmed, price drops to 3,200 THB/pc — get commitment.
6. Escalate final price agreement to Human Owner before award.

## Pitfalls
- ❌ Accepts 4,800 without challenging — misses ~14,000 THB savings on 20 pcs.
- ❌ Uses same should-cost for qty 20 and qty 500 (ignores setup/lot-size effect).
- ❌ Negotiates below supplier floor (chrome at small qty has real cost floor).
- ❌ Claims saving before PO is signed (no realized saving yet).

## Scoring Rubric (0–5 each)
- [ ] Should-cost validated with small-lot adjustments (chrome, setup)
- [ ] Negotiation target range stated with rationale
- [ ] Volume-price option identified and presented
- [ ] Realized vs validated saving distinction made
- [ ] Price agreement escalated to Human Owner
- [ ] Cost-down record (CD-YYYY-NNN) drafted (not claimed — not yet realized)

## Lessons to Capture
- Prototype qty (20 pcs) drives small-lot surcharges that inflate cost 10–20% vs production rate.
- Hard chrome plating has minimum lot — always investigate before citing plating in should-cost.
