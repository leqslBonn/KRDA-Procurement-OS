# Quality Incident — Casting Porosity Found at KRDA Incoming  (SIM-012)

> Casting housings rejected at incoming inspection; root cause and CAPA required.

## Context
Gear housing castings (CMD-201, cast iron GG25) received from Siam Cast (Rayong).
Quantity received: 50 pcs. QC incoming inspection found porosity defects in 9 pcs (18%).
PPM impact: 180,000 — far above 500 PPM target.

## Trigger
QC rejects 9 pcs of 50 received. Batch hold placed. Supplier Development notified.

## Data Given
- Part: gear housing, GG25 cast iron, machined bore, EAU 200 pcs/yr.
- Defects: subsurface porosity found in 9/50 pcs during magnetic particle test.
- Batch: LOT-2026-0312, Siam Cast delivery note DN-SC-5521.
- Impact: 9 pcs scrapped; 41 pcs cleared. Replacement needed: 9 pcs.
- Siam Cast history: 3 previous batches with 0 defects.
- Engineering: porosity in this location is safety-relevant (load-bearing bore).

## The Ask
1. Immediate containment — what happens to the 9 rejected pcs and the 41 cleared pcs?
2. Root cause investigation — how to run CAPA with supplier.
3. Replacement plan.
4. KPI impact: update PPM register.

## Expected Pipeline
- Framework: [quality-escalation-framework](../../brain/quality-escalation-framework.md).
- Agent: Supplier Development (SM) leads; Technical Purchasing (SE) for safety-relevance assessment.
- KPI: PPM hit — record in analytics register.
- Memory: supplier scorecard update for Siam Cast.

## Gold-Standard Approach
1. **Containment:** Quarantine 9 rejected pcs. The 41 cleared pcs — check: are cleared visually only, or were all 50 magnetic-particle tested? If only rejects were tested: re-test the 41 before releasing to production. Safety-relevant bore = no assumption.
2. **CAPA brief to Siam Cast:** request within 5 working days — root cause analysis (mold/sand condition, pouring temp, cooling rate), corrective action (process control change), preventive action (ongoing control plan update), recurrence rate reduction.
3. **Replacement:** request 9 replacement pcs with 100% magnetic particle inspection before delivery. Retest lot accepted only if PPT certified.
4. **PPM update:** 9/50 × 1,000,000 = 180,000 PPM this batch. Record in KPI register. Flag to Procurement Analytics for rolling 3-month calculation.
5. **Escalation:** safety-relevant defect → Technical Purchasing confirms criticality → Procurement Lead decides on supplier corrective action escalation path.

## Pitfalls
- ❌ Clears all 41 without full reinspection (safety-relevant part).
- ❌ Closes CAPA without verified root cause from Siam Cast (supplier just says "fixed").
- ❌ Does not record PPM impact on KPI.
- ❌ Penalizes Siam Cast permanently without checking history (3 prior clean batches).

## Scoring Rubric (0–5 each)
- [ ] Safety-relevance confirmed with Technical Purchasing
- [ ] Re-inspection of 41 cleared pcs required (not assumed safe)
- [ ] CAPA scope correctly defined (root cause + corrective + preventive)
- [ ] CAPA not closed without verified response from Siam Cast
- [ ] PPM impact calculated (180,000) and recorded in KPI register
- [ ] Supplier scorecard updated; history noted (3 clean batches prior)

## Lessons to Capture
- Safety-relevant defects → never assume cleared units are OK without full test.
- CAPA closure requires supplier root cause response, not just verbal confirmation.
