# Engineering change, mid-production  (SIM-004)

> An ECN changes a material mid-production; assess full purchasing impact and cut-in.

## Context
Cast housing, supplier G (qualified). Engineering issues ECN: material change for
durability, affecting tooling and possibly re-qualification.

## Trigger
ECN released, rev A→B, requested cut-in in 6 weeks.

## Data Given
- Old-stock on hand: ~3 weeks. New material may need tooling tweak + first-article.
- Price impact unknown; supplier claims +tooling cost.

## The Ask
Assess all impacts and recommend a cut-in plan.

## Expected Pipeline
- Framework: [engineering-change-framework](../../brain/engineering-change-framework.md).
- Workflow [engineering_change.md](../../workflows/engineering_change.md); may call
  [supplier_audit](../../workflows/supplier_audit.md), [price_negotiation](../../workflows/price_negotiation.md), [sap](../../workflows/sap.md).

## Gold-Standard Approach
Run the six-dimension impact (technical, supplier, cost, sourcing, transaction,
timing) — **none skipped**. Sequence: re-qual (first article) → price agreement →
master-data update → cut-in after old-stock run-out to avoid obsolescence.
**Escalate cut-in + any price increase to Human Owner.**

## Pitfalls
- ❌ Cuts in before re-qualification / first article.
- ❌ Ignores old-stock obsolescence cost.
- ❌ Skips a department's impact assessment.

## Scoring Rubric (0–5 each)
- [ ] All six impacts assessed
- [ ] Re-qual sequenced before cut-in
- [ ] Obsolescence quantified
- [ ] Price impact negotiated
- [ ] Cut-in escalated to Human Owner
- [ ] Lesson captured

## Lessons to Capture
- Cut-in timing vs old-stock; tooling-change lead time for cast parts.
