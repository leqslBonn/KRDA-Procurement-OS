# Supplier selection — capacity constraint  (SIM-030)

> Demand is rising; the incumbent supplier is near capacity. Add a source.

## Context
Demand for a machined part grows 40%; incumbent (qualified, good) signals limited capacity
headroom.

## Data Given
- Incumbent quality strong but capacity-limited; lead time slipping.
- Two candidates with capacity; one qualified for other parts, one new.

## The Ask
Select an additional/alternative source.

## Expected Pipeline
- [supplier-selection-framework](../../brain/supplier-selection-framework.md), [supplier-risk-framework](../../brain/supplier-risk-framework.md); [supplier_selection](../../workflows/supplier_selection.md) + audit new candidate.

## Gold-Standard Approach
Score candidates with **capacity headroom weighted** (it's the binding constraint). Keep
incumbent for its quality; add capacity via the qualified candidate; qualify the new one as
backup. Avoid over-fragmenting. Award split = Human Owner (D3). Reduces delivery risk.

## Pitfalls
- ❌ Replacing a strong incumbent instead of supplementing.
- ❌ Ignoring capacity as the key criterion this time.

## Scoring Rubric (0–5 each)
- [ ] Capacity weighted in scoring · [ ] Incumbent retained for quality · [ ] New source qualified
- [ ] Split justified · [ ] Escalated · [ ] Lesson captured

## Lessons to Capture
- Criteria weights shift with the binding constraint (capacity here).
