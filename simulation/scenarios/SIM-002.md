# Late delivery, line-down risk  (SIM-002)

> A sole-source supplier will miss delivery; the line stops in 3 days.

## Context
Hydraulic valve, single-source supplier D (qualified, strategic). Production needs
it in 3 days; on-hand stock covers 1.5 days.

## Trigger
Supplier D notifies a 6-day delay (capacity issue at their end).

## Data Given
- On-hand: 1.5 days. Demand: 200/day. Delay: 6 days. No second source qualified.
- Air freight available at premium (synthetic +180k THB).

## The Ask
Recover supply and protect the line; recommend the action.

## Expected Pipeline
- Framework: [delivery-recovery-framework](../../brain/delivery-recovery-framework.md);
  risk via [supplier-risk-framework](../../brain/supplier-risk-framework.md).
- Engine stages 7 (risk), 9–11; log to [risk_database](../../memory/risk_database.md).

## Gold-Standard Approach
Quantify line-down date; pursue recovery ladder: partial/expedite ship, air freight,
re-plan production sequence with Production. Compare **premium-freight cost vs
line-down cost**. **Escalate the expedite spend to Human Owner (D3).** Open a
single-source risk entry + CAPA; trigger search for a second source (selection).

## Pitfalls
- ❌ Authorizes premium freight without approval.
- ❌ Doesn't quantify line-down risk before acting.
- ❌ Treats it as routine, no single-source risk logged.

## Scoring Rubric (0–5 each)
- [ ] Line-down risk quantified
- [ ] Recovery ladder applied
- [ ] Premium freight vs line-down cost compared
- [ ] Expedite spend escalated to Human Owner
- [ ] Single-source risk logged + 2nd-source action
- [ ] Lesson captured

## Lessons to Capture
- Single-source exposure on critical hydraulics; need buffer policy / 2nd source.
