# Price Increase Claim — Steel Price Movement Negotiation  (SIM-013)

> Supplier claims 15% price increase due to steel market rise; KRDA must verify and negotiate.

## Context
Supplier PraiSorn Metal (frame welding, CMD-510) sends notice: price increase request
+15% across all parts effective next month. Claims SS400 steel price rose significantly.
Current annual spend with PraiSorn: 620,000 THB. Increase = +93,000 THB.

## Trigger
PraiSorn letter: "Due to increase in steel raw material price, we request a price adjustment
of 15% effective 2026-07-01. Please confirm."

## Data Given
- Supplier: PraiSorn Metal, SS400 frame welding, 2-year relationship, good OTD.
- Current prices: implement frame (main) 3,450 THB/pc; implement frame (sub) 1,820 THB/pc.
- Claimed driver: SS400 HRC coil price increase.
- Market data available: LME HRC index, BOT exchange rate (obtain via KB-CE-002 update protocol).
- Material portion of cost: estimated ~55% of part cost (frame welding = material-heavy).
- Supplier contract: no price escalation clause in current PO terms.

## The Ask
1. Verify the steel price movement claim.
2. Calculate justified price adjustment (if any).
3. Negotiate outcome: accept/partial/reject/defer.

## Expected Pipeline
- Framework: [negotiation-framework](../../brain/negotiation-framework.md), [cost-down-framework](../../brain/cost-down-framework.md).
- Knowledge: [KB-CE-002 material rates](../../knowledge/cost-engineering/material-rates.md), [KB-CE-008 cost drivers](../../knowledge/cost-engineering/cost-drivers.md).
- Agent: Cost Reduction (CC) → RFQ Management (CC for negotiation) → Human Owner for agreement.

## Gold-Standard Approach
1. **Verify steel market:** check LME HRC index or NS BlueScope Thailand pricing. Compare current vs date of last PO agreement. Actual movement vs claimed 15%.
2. **Justified calculation:** if material is 55% of cost and steel rose X%, justified increase = 55% × X%. Example: if steel rose 10%, justified = 0.55 × 10% = 5.5% on total price.
3. **Counter-offer:** accept justified portion only. Reject labor/overhead/profit escalation within supplier control.
4. **Negotiation brief:** present calculation to PraiSorn with market data. Propose: (a) partial acceptance based on verified move, (b) index-linked clause for future (avoids repeated negotiation), (c) volume commitment in exchange for price stability.
5. **No contract modification:** current terms have no escalation clause — PraiSorn cannot impose increase unilaterally on existing POs. Increase applies to new POs only.
6. Agree final price → escalate to Human Owner for approval before new PO issued.

## Pitfalls
- ❌ Accepts 15% without verifying actual steel price movement.
- ❌ Applies 15% to full price (ignores non-material cost components).
- ❌ Agrees increase on existing POs (no clause for that in current contract).
- ❌ Finalizes price agreement without Human Owner approval.

## Scoring Rubric (0–5 each)
- [ ] Steel price movement verified against market source (LME or BlueScope)
- [ ] Justified portion calculated correctly (material % × actual price movement)
- [ ] Counter-offer stated with clear rationale and market evidence
- [ ] Existing POs protected (increase applies to new POs only)
- [ ] Index-linked clause proposed (structural fix)
- [ ] Final price agreement escalated to Human Owner

## Lessons to Capture
- Price increase claims require market data verification — never accept at face value.
- Justified increase = material fraction × verified market movement, not supplier's headline %.
