# Supplier price-increase demand  (SIM-003)

> A supplier demands +12%, citing raw-material rises. Defend with should-cost.

## Context
Stamped part, supplier E (qualified), running price 30 THB/pc, EAU 120,000.

## Trigger
Supplier E sends a +12% price-increase notice effective next quarter.

## Data Given
- Current 30 THB; demand 33.6 THB. Steel is ~55% of cost (synthetic).
- Steel index up ~8% over the period. Alternative supplier F is qualified.

## The Ask
Decide whether/how much increase is justified; respond.

## Expected Pipeline
- Frameworks: [cost-down-framework](../../brain/cost-down-framework.md),
  [negotiation-framework](../../brain/negotiation-framework.md).
- Skill: [cost_breakdown](../../skills/cost_breakdown.md); workflow [price_negotiation](../../workflows/price_negotiation.md).

## Gold-Standard Approach
Rebuild should-cost: only the **material portion** (55%) is exposed to the ~8% steel
rise → justified increase ≈ 55% × 8% ≈ 4.4%, not 12%. Anchor the counter near +4–5%;
use supplier F as BATNA. **Escalate the agreed price to Human Owner.** Never disclose
F's quote to E.

## Pitfalls
- ❌ Accepts +12% at face value.
- ❌ Applies the index to the whole price, not just material.
- ❌ Leaks F's quote for leverage.

## Scoring Rubric (0–5 each)
- [ ] Should-cost isolates material exposure
- [ ] Justified increase computed (~4–5%, not 12%)
- [ ] BATNA defined
- [ ] Confidentiality preserved
- [ ] Agreed price escalated to Human Owner
- [ ] Lesson captured

## Lessons to Capture
- Index pass-through math; keep material-share data per commodity in cost-engineering.
