# New supplier — capability gap  (SIM-033)

> A promising new machining supplier passes most of the audit but fails a critical gate.

## Context
New CNC supplier, attractive price/lead time. Audit ([checklist](../../knowledge/supplier-management/audit-checklist.md)) finds no
calibration program and weak SPC.

## Data Given
- Strong equipment, good price; but quality-system gaps (calibration, SPC) — critical-ish.
- Willing to improve.

## The Ask
Decide qualification status and path.

## Expected Pipeline
- [supplier_audit](../../workflows/supplier_audit.md); [supplier-risk-framework](../../brain/supplier-risk-framework.md).

## Gold-Standard Approach
Per checklist gates, calibration/SPC gaps → **conditional**, not qualified. Set CAPA (build
calibration program + SPC on key characteristics) with due dates; re-audit. Do not award
critical parts until closed; could trial on low-risk parts. Status decision recorded; any
development spend = Human Owner (D3).

## Pitfalls
- ❌ Qualifying on price despite a critical quality-system gap.
- ❌ Awarding critical parts before CAPA closure.

## Scoring Rubric (0–5 each)
- [ ] Gates applied (calibration/SPC) · [ ] Conditional status + CAPA · [ ] Re-audit planned
- [ ] No critical award pre-closure · [ ] Spend escalated if any · [ ] Lesson captured

## Lessons to Capture
- Quality-system gates over price; develop conditional suppliers methodically.
