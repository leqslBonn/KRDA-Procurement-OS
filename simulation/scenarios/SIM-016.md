# Localization — import fitting to local source  (SIM-016)

> An imported (JP) hydraulic fitting is costly + FX-exposed; evaluate localizing to a Thai supplier.

## Context
Fitting imported from Japan, priced in JPY, EAU 20,000. FX and lead time hurt. A Thai
supplier claims it can make to print.

## Data Given
- Import price ¥/pc → ~78 THB at 0.235 JPY/THB; 8-wk lead; MOQ + freight + duty.
- Thai quote (synthetic) ~62 THB, 3-wk lead, needs qualification.

## The Ask
Decide whether to localize; recommend.

## Expected Pipeline
- Frameworks: [make-vs-buy](../../brain/make-vs-buy.md) (insource/region), [cost-down-framework](../../brain/cost-down-framework.md) (localization lever), [supplier-risk-framework](../../brain/supplier-risk-framework.md).
- Workflows: [supplier_selection](../../workflows/supplier_selection.md) → [supplier_audit](../../workflows/supplier_audit.md) → [rfq](../../workflows/rfq.md).

## Gold-Standard Approach
Compare **TCO** (price + FX risk + freight + duty + lead/inventory) not unit price alone.
Qualify the Thai source ([nda](../../workflows/nda.md) before sharing the print; audit).
Localizing cuts ~20% + FX exposure + lead — but **gate on qualification & first-article**.
Escalate the switch (D3) + any tooling spend to Human Owner.

## Pitfalls
- ❌ Switching on price before qualification/first-article.
- ❌ Ignoring FX risk reduction as a benefit.
- ❌ Sharing the print before NDA.

## Scoring Rubric (0–5 each)
- [ ] TCO compared (not unit price)
- [ ] Qualification + first-article gated before switch
- [ ] FX/lead benefits quantified
- [ ] NDA before print shared
- [ ] Switch escalated to Human Owner
- [ ] Lesson captured

## Lessons to Capture
- Localization TCO model; FX exposure as a cost driver.
