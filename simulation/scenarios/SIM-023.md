# Cost down — stamped guard  (SIM-023)

> A stamped guard panel is over should-cost; material utilization is the lever.

## Context
SPCC guard panel, EAU 15,000, price 58 THB. Drawing yields poor sheet nesting (high scrap).

## Data Given
- Should-cost ≈ 48 THB; current scrap ~35% vs typical 18% → material waste is the gap.
- Re-nest / minor flat-pattern change could cut scrap.

## The Ask
Find and prove the saving.

## Expected Pipeline
- [cost_down](../../workflows/cost_down.md); [cost-down-framework](../../brain/cost-down-framework.md); skill [cost_breakdown](../../skills/cost_breakdown.md); spec tweak → [engineering_change](../../workflows/engineering_change.md).

## Gold-Standard Approach
Should-cost isolates excess scrap. Lever = VA/VE on flat pattern + supplier nesting
optimization (route any spec change via engineering_change). Validate saving vs baseline;
realization = Human Owner (D3). Update [yield-scrap](../../knowledge/cost-engineering/yield-scrap.md) learning.

## Pitfalls
- ❌ Negotiating price without fixing the scrap root cause.
- ❌ Changing the part outline without engineering sign-off.

## Scoring Rubric (0–5 each)
- [ ] Scrap quantified vs benchmark · [ ] Lever targets root cause · [ ] Spec change routed
- [ ] Saving validated · [ ] Realization escalated · [ ] Lesson captured

## Lessons to Capture
- Nesting/scrap as a cost driver in stamping.
