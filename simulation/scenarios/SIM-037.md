# NDA — overly broad terms  (SIM-037)

> A supplier returns the NDA with broadened scope and a long perpetual term. Review.

## Context
Onboarding a technology supplier; they redline the NDA to cover "all KRDA business" with a
perpetual term and weak return-of-information clause.

## Data Given
- Proposed scope far wider than the actual disclosure (one part program).
- Perpetual term; no information-return/destruction clause.

## The Ask
Review and recommend before the Human Owner signs.

## Expected Pipeline
- [nda](../../workflows/nda.md); [nda-review template](../../templates/nda-review.md); [approval-matrix](../../brain/approval-matrix.md).

## Gold-Standard Approach
Narrow scope to the actual program; cap term (e.g., 3–5 yrs); add return/destruction clause;
keep mutual. Prepare a redline + recommendation; **Human Owner signs** (legal, D3). Don't
accept perpetual/over-broad terms by default.

## Pitfalls
- ❌ Accepting over-broad scope/perpetual term as-is.
- ❌ Missing the return-of-information clause.
- ❌ AI agreeing terms instead of preparing for signature.

## Scoring Rubric (0–5 each)
- [ ] Scope narrowed to need · [ ] Term capped · [ ] Return clause added
- [ ] Redline prepared · [ ] Human Owner signs · [ ] Lesson captured

## Lessons to Capture
- NDA scope/term hygiene; standard redlines.
