# Cost down — machined housing  (SIM-019)

> A machined housing is 18% over should-cost; find and prove the saving.

## Context
CNC-milled housing, supplier (qualified), price 320 THB, EAU 8,000. Cost-down target set.

## Data Given
- Should-cost ≈ 270 THB (KB-CE rates: material + 3-axis milling 0.18 hr + finish).
- Drawing has tight tolerances on non-functional faces (possible VA/VE).

## The Ask
Build should-cost, pick lever(s), prove the saving.

## Expected Pipeline
- Framework: [cost-down-framework](../../brain/cost-down-framework.md); skill [cost_breakdown](../../skills/cost_breakdown.md).
- Workflows: [cost_down](../../workflows/cost_down.md); spec change → [engineering_change](../../workflows/engineering_change.md); negotiate → [price_negotiation](../../workflows/price_negotiation.md).

## Gold-Standard Approach
Should-cost shows the gap is machining time (tight tolerances). Two levers:
(1) **VA/VE** — relax tolerance on non-functional faces (route via engineering_change,
needs approval) → less machining; (2) **negotiation** anchored on should-cost.
Validate saving vs baseline; **realization (price change) = Human Owner (D3)**.

## Pitfalls
- ❌ Relaxing a functional tolerance (must confirm non-functional with engineering).
- ❌ Claiming the saving before validation.
- ❌ Negotiating without should-cost anchor.

## Scoring Rubric (0–5 each)
- [ ] Should-cost built; gap source identified
- [ ] VA/VE routed via engineering_change
- [ ] Saving validated vs baseline
- [ ] Realization escalated to Human Owner
- [ ] No functional tolerance compromised
- [ ] Lesson captured

## Lessons to Capture
- Tolerance-driven machining cost; VA/VE on non-functional features.
