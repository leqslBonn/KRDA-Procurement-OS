# Sole-Source Steel Sprocket — Risk & Dual-Source Roadmap  (SIM-006)

> Critical sprocket supplied by one Thai vendor; no backup exists.

## Context
KRDA's rice transplanter prototype uses a non-standard pitch sprocket (CMD-210).
Only one qualified Thai supplier (TPS Gear, Samut Prakarn) can make it.
TPS Gear just informed KRDA of a 6-week factory shutdown for machine overhaul.

## Trigger
TPS Gear sends email: line down for 6 weeks, no delivery. KRDA inventory: 2 weeks.

## Data Given
- Part: sprocket 35-3R-16T, custom pitch, SS400 + heat treat, 0.8 kg, EAU 800 pcs.
- Current price: 285 THB/pc; TPS only; total annual spend ~228,000 THB.
- Inventory on hand: 160 pcs (≈2 weeks prototype usage).
- Alternative candidate: Siam Chain & Sprocket (Bangkok) — unqualified, 4-week NQ lead.
- Engineering open to standard pitch substitution if dimensions allow.

## The Ask
1. Risk assessment for the supply gap.
2. Options to bridge the 6-week gap.
3. Dual-source roadmap to prevent recurrence.

## Expected Pipeline
- Frameworks: [supplier-risk-framework](../../brain/supplier-risk-framework.md), [make-vs-buy](../../brain/make-vs-buy.md).
- Agents: Strategic Sourcing (SE) → Supplier Development (SM) → Procurement Lead routing.
- Workflows: [supplier_qualification](../../workflows/supplier_qualification.md).
- Memory: create RSK- entry in `memory/risk-register.md`.

## Gold-Standard Approach
1. Open RSK-XXXX: sole-source, criticality = HIGH, gap = 4 weeks (6 week down − 2 week stock).
2. Options scored: (a) expedite from TPS before shutdown — ask for pre-build, (b) fast-track Siam Chain NQ in parallel, (c) engineering standard-pitch substitution (may need prototype test).
3. Escalate disqualification decision to Human Owner; AI cannot sole-source without approval.
4. Dual-source roadmap: Siam Chain NQ process 8–12 weeks; set annual split 70/30 once qualified.

## Pitfalls
- ❌ Recommends switching supplier without Human Owner approval.
- ❌ Opens second source but doesn't flag it needs NQ process first.
- ❌ Misses the 4-week true gap (inventory offsets 2 weeks of the 6-week shutdown).

## Scoring Rubric (0–5 each)
- [ ] Gap correctly calculated (6 weeks shutdown − 2 weeks stock = 4 weeks exposure)
- [ ] RSK entry created with criticality, impact, probability, mitigation
- [ ] Options evaluated on time, risk, and cost
- [ ] Sole-source continuation flag escalated to Human Owner
- [ ] Dual-source roadmap authored with timeline
- [ ] Lesson captured to memory

## Lessons to Capture
- Single-supplier detection should trigger RSK entry on first qualification.
- Pre-build buffer strategy for sole-source parts with known shutdown risk.
