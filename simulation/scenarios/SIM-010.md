# Engineering Change — Blade Material S45C → S55C  (SIM-010)

> Engineering requests material upgrade for a blade; procurement must update spec, requote, and validate cost impact.

## Context
KRDA field test shows blade wear rate too high on S45C (HRC 45). Engineering proposes
upgrade to S55C (target HRC 55 with induction hardening). Drawing controlled at Rev A;
upgrade will issue Rev B. Same geometry, new material + process spec.

## Trigger
Engineering Change Notice ECN-2026-015 issued: blade material S45C → S55C, add induction
hardening step. Cut-in: next prototype build (8 pcs). Technical Purchasing must process.

## Data Given
- Part: harvester blade, current Rev A S45C, 2.1 kg, 8 pcs prototype.
- New spec: S55C + induction hardening (surface 55–60 HRC).
- Current supplier: Siam Blade Co. — qualified for S45C; can they do S55C + induction?
- Alternative process: hardfacing (overlay weld) — different cost structure, may be cheaper.
- Current price (S45C): 2,850 THB/pc. Target (S55C): Engineering has no cost target.
- Lead time impact: unknown.

## The Ask
1. Confirm supplier capability for Rev B spec.
2. Should-cost estimate for S55C + induction vs hardfacing alternative.
3. Requote Siam Blade; evaluate if alternative process makes sense.
4. Update SAP material master after drawing release.

## Expected Pipeline
- Framework: [engineering-change-framework](../../brain/engineering-change-framework.md), [technical-purchasing-framework](../../brain/technical-purchasing-framework.md).
- Agents: Technical Purchasing (SE) leads; Cost Reduction (CC) builds should-cost.
- Knowledge: [KB-CE-001](../../knowledge/cost-engineering/should-cost-method.md), [KB-CE-002](../../knowledge/cost-engineering/material-rates.md).
- Workflow: [rfq.md](../../workflows/rfq.md) for requote.

## Gold-Standard Approach
1. Review ECN-2026-015: scope confirmed (material + hardening only, geometry unchanged).
2. Capability check with Siam Blade: S55C machining ✅ (standard steel), induction hardening — sub-contract likely.
3. Should-cost comparison:
   - S55C + induction: material +12% (S55C > S45C), induction sub-contract ~400–600 THB → est. 3,500–3,800 THB.
   - Hardfacing alternative: similar or lower cost but requires different supplier — evaluate separately.
4. Requote Siam Blade against Rev B drawing. Request lead time impact.
5. If Siam Blade cannot do induction in-house: assess sub-contractor chain risk.
6. Award Rev B to Human Owner approval before cutting new PO.
7. SAP: update material master after Rev B drawing officially released.

## Pitfalls
- ❌ Issues PO against old Rev A drawing after ECN (wrong revision cut-in).
- ❌ Compares S45C price to S55C quote without acknowledging material cost difference.
- ❌ Updates SAP before drawing officially released (ECN stage, not released).
- ❌ Skips capability check — assumes Siam Blade can do induction hardening in-house.

## Scoring Rubric (0–5 each)
- [ ] ECN reviewed; scope understood (geometry unchanged, material + process change only)
- [ ] Supplier capability for induction hardening confirmed (not assumed)
- [ ] Should-cost built for new spec; variance vs current price quantified
- [ ] Process alternative (hardfacing) evaluated with cost comparison
- [ ] Rev B award escalated to Human Owner
- [ ] SAP update deferred until official drawing release

## Lessons to Capture
- Material change always triggers capability check — don't assume current supplier can do new spec.
- ECN cut-in must align with drawing release; SAP update follows drawing, not ECN issue date.
