# RFQ — gearbox housing  (SIM-021)

> Quote a cast-then-machined gearbox housing across mixed-maturity suppliers.

## Context
CHS gearbox housing, grey-iron casting + CNC machining. EAU 6,000. Two qualified
casters, one new entrant with aggressive price.

## Data Given
- Should-cost ≈ 410 THB (casting 75 THB/kg × 3.2 kg + machining 0.3 hr + finish).
- Quotes (synthetic): A 425 (qualified), B 440 (qualified), C 380 (new, unproven).

## The Ask
Run RFQ to award recommendation.

## Expected Pipeline
- [rfq](../../workflows/rfq.md); frameworks [rfq-evaluation](../../brain/rfq-evaluation-framework.md), [technical-purchasing](../../brain/technical-purchasing-framework.md), [supplier-risk](../../brain/supplier-risk-framework.md).

## Gold-Standard Approach
Normalize quotes (casting + machining + freight). C cheapest but **conditional on
qualification** ([supplier_audit](../../workflows/supplier_audit.md) + first-article) — do not
award on price before qualification. Recommend A (below should-cost, qualified) unless C
qualifies in time. Award = Human Owner (D3).

## Pitfalls
- ❌ Awarding C on price before qualification.
- ❌ Not separating casting vs machining cost in comparison.

## Scoring Rubric (0–5 each)
- [ ] Quotes normalized · [ ] Tech/qual gate applied · [ ] Variance vs should-cost
- [ ] Risk on new supplier flagged · [ ] Award escalated to Human Owner · [ ] Lesson captured

## Lessons to Capture
- New-supplier price vs qualification timing trade-off.
