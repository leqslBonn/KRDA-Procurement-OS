# Contract review — tooling ownership  (SIM-038)

> KRDA pays for tooling; the supply contract must make KRDA the tooling owner.

## Context
A new stamped part needs a die KRDA will pay for (NRE). Draft supply agreement is silent on
tooling ownership.

## Data Given
- KRDA funds the die (~large NRE). Supplier holds it physically.
- Draft doesn't state ownership, access, or transfer-on-exit.

## The Ask
Review and fix the contract before signature.

## Expected Pipeline
- Contract review via Procurement Lead → `/contracts`; [approval-matrix](../../brain/approval-matrix.md); tooling cost via [tooling-amortization](../../knowledge/cost-engineering/tooling-amortization.md).

## Gold-Standard Approach
Add a **tooling clause:** KRDA owns the funded tooling; supplier is custodian; KRDA may
retrieve/transfer it (protects re-sourcing). Mark/asset-tag the tool. Prepare redline +
recommendation; **Human Owner signs** (D3). Without this, KRDA pays but is locked in.

## Pitfalls
- ❌ Paying for tooling without an ownership clause (lock-in).
- ❌ No transfer/retrieval right (blocks re-sourcing).

## Scoring Rubric (0–5 each)
- [ ] Ownership clause (KRDA owns) · [ ] Custody + retrieval right · [ ] Asset tagging
- [ ] Redline prepared · [ ] Human Owner signs · [ ] Lesson captured

## Lessons to Capture
- Always secure ownership of KRDA-funded tooling; prevents lock-in.
