## 1. Scope & Policy

- Covers all procurement from domestic and international sources, including land.
- Goal: efficient, systematic, cautious for KRDA interests — price, quality, date, timeliness.
- **Persons with procurement authority** are assigned by departments:
  - **Finance/Accounting:** loans, service, tax.
  - **HR & Admin:** training/termination/temp staff, hospital/duty doctors, transport providers.
  - **ASEAN Marketing Center Division:** advertising, media, contract design, research.
  - **R&D and Innovation (HQ):** field test equipment, GL 61026201.00.
  - **Utility:** maintenance/urgent service.
  - **Safety:** urgent safety materials/services.

### Procurement Policies

1. Maximize benefit on price, quality, date, and timely delivery.
2. Procure from reliable suppliers, dealers, or operators.
3. Avoid unnecessary procurement (within 3-month excess stock rule).
4. Refrain from suppliers with fraudulent intent or Company-damaging behavior.
5. Avoid personal-benefit procurement — refer to Item 3.1 in the policy.
6. Procure via the Procurement Section except where exceptions apply (Item 3.1).
7. Opt for domestic products meeting industrial standards.
8. Build and maintain good supplier relationships.
9. Do not support functions of direct users for self-procurement, except as stated.

## 2. Types of Procurement

### 2.1 General Procurement (via Procurement Section)

All standard purchases of materials, services, and prototype parts.

### 2.2 Direct Procurement (per function, no Procurement Section)

Allowed for urgent or routine needs **not exceeding 3,000 THB per receipt**.

#### Direct Purchase Approval Authority (TH v2 — authoritative)

| Amount (THB) | Approver |
|---|---|
| 0 – 10,000 | Division Manager |
| 10,001 – 20,000 | Vice President (VP) |
| > 20,000 | President |

#### Direct from SRC or SKC Authorized Dealer (urgent)

| Amount (THB) | Approver |
|---|---|
| 0 – 50,000 | Department Manager |
| > 50,000 | Division Manager |

### 2.3 Prototype Parts

Procured through the Procurement Section; lead times and specs sourced from requester.

## 3. Procurement Methods (Chapter 3)

| Method | Amount/Item (THB) | Price Inquiry | Min. Suppliers |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Price Agreement** | 0 – 5,000 | Verbal | ≥ 1 |
| **Price Comparison** | 5,001 – 1,000,000 | Written | ≥ 2 |
| **Bidding** | > 1,000,000 | Written + formal tender | ≥ 3 |

### Price Agreement (≤ 5,000 THB)

- Single supplier verbal inquiry allowed.
- Price from latest procurement may be used if no benefit of re-comparison.
- Must document: supplier, price, date, reason for selection.

### Price Comparison (5,001 – 1,000,000 THB)

- Written inquiry to ≥ 2 suppliers.
- Price comparison form submitted to procurement authority.
- Exception (single supplier): made for >3 months, labor cost, specific unique supplier, etc.

### Bidding (> 1,000,000 THB)

- Bidding notification to ≥ 3 suppliers with: spec, qty, date, delivery place, conditions.
- Bid bond required: % of order determined by procurement authority; returnable in 7 days.
- Bidding Committee (≥ 3 persons): 1 from General Purchasing, 2 from other functions.
- Ad-Hoc Committee available for specialist input.
- Opening: all Committee members present (or written proxy).
- Lowest bid considered; if materials/conditions unmet, second-lowest may be selected.
- Bidding may be postponed by procurement authority if necessary.
- Bid cancellation: allowed if Company has low benefit, or supplier no longer required.

## 5. Contract or Purchase Order (Chapter 6)

### When a Contract is Used

- Payment before delivery OR pre-payment > 200,000 THB with lead time > 30 days.
- Total amount > 400,000 THB with lead time > 60 days.
- Scope of responsibility for goods/services must be signed by both parties.

### Contract Exemption

Requires President approval via written memo explaining reason.

### Purchase Order (PO)

- PO issued for all accepted procurement (after approval).
- PO also issued for expenses ≤ 5,000 THB when subject to procurement rules.
- PO is the Company's acceptance of quotation: supplier, price, payment, delivery.

## 6. Vendor/Service Provider Selection (Chapter 8)

- New vendor must be evaluated: financial status, production capacity, quality, service, price.
- Preferred: vendors already on the approved vendor list.
- Vendor performance tracked by: delivery, quality reject rate, service response.

---

## 7. Receiving Process (Chapter 9)

- Receiving = inspection of materials/services per procurement method.
- Receiving authority = procurement officer + function requester (for specialist knowledge).
- Analytical testing allowed when specific knowledge required.

---

## 8. Selling of Used Materials (Chapters 10–11)

- Price comparison or bidding (≥ 500,000 THB) applies.
- Conditions for selling: all 3 methods allowed; no limits on amounts.
- Special methods: sold to SRC/SKC group or case-by-case special buyers.

---

## 9. Non-Eligible Expenses (via Procurement Section)

The following are handled **directly by the function** (not Procurement):
- Training/activity expenses within approved budget.
- Gasoline, ferry service, bank charges.
- Taxi fares.
- Urgent photocopies.
- Miscellaneous expenses outside normal hours.
- Field test rental.
- Procurement from SRC, SKC, KRT (legal services).
- Transport arranged by Japanese staff.
- Agricultural produce direct from farmers.
- Vehicle maintenance (handled by respective section).

---

## 10. Contracting Rules

- Contract must be in Thai (and English version is acceptable if applicable).
- Stored in Law Office; copy in Procurement Section and requesting department.
- Earnest money: held until contract execution + 7 days after successful delivery.
- Stamp duty required per Revenue Code; endorsement slip acceptable alternative.

## 12. Competitor Product Procurement (TH v2 — Appendix 2 & 4)

> Added in TH v2 (Jan 2023). Not in EN v1.

- KRDA may procure competitor products for R&D benchmarking purposes only.
- Requires **President (KRDA) approval** before proceeding.
- Must use a **supplier not in KRDA's regular supplier list** (to avoid disclosure).
- Must NOT identify as KRDA when purchasing competitor products.
- Full documentation of purpose, authorization, and disposition required.
- Managed as a special procurement case — not through normal price comparison flow.

## 13. New Vendor Registration (TH v2 — Appendix 5 & 6)

> Added in TH v2 (Jan 2023). Not in EN v1.

### Process (3 stages)

**Stage 1 — Vendor Profiling**
1. PU sends Vendor Registration Form to prospective supplier (via website or QR code).
2. Supplier completes **Vendor Master Form**: company name, address, tax ID, bank details, contacts.
3. Supplier provides: Por.Por.20 (VAT cert), company certificate (DBD), authorized signatory stamp.

**Stage 2 — Check Creditworthiness**
1. PU downloads company status / VAT registration from: `www.dbd.go.th` / `www.rd.go.th`
2. PU verifies supplier is actively operating (not ceased/dissolved).
3. PU confirms all data matches submitted documents.
4. If discrepancy → supplier must correct and resubmit.

**Stage 3 — Create & Maintain in SAP**
1. PU creates/changes vendor form in SAP system.
2. All documents archived: Vendor Master Form, Por.Por.20, company cert, bank info, time-clearing scan.
3. Accounting & PU co-approve vendor creation in SAP.
