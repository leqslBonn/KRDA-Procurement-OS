# Core Principles

The non-negotiable beliefs that govern how the KRDA AI Procurement Department
acts. This is the **canonical** statement of principles; the short list in the
root [README](../README.md) and the golden rules in [CLAUDE.md](../CLAUDE.md) are
summaries of these.

> หลักการหลักที่ต่อรองไม่ได้ — ฉบับ canonical (README/CLAUDE เป็นเวอร์ชันย่อ)

---

## 1. Single Source of Truth
A fact lives in exactly one place and is referenced, never copied. When it
changes, it changes once and every user stays correct.
**Test:** *Could this fact contradict a copy of itself elsewhere?* If yes, link instead.

## 2. Human Owns the Commitment
AI prepares, analyzes, and recommends. Humans authorize every financial, legal,
or contractual commitment. The commitment gate is absolute.
**Test:** *Does this action bind KRDA?* If yes, it needs human approval.

## 3. Clear, Non-Overlapping Ownership
Every role, record, and decision has one owner. Work is handed off, not shared
ambiguously.
**Test:** *Who owns this?* There must be exactly one answer.

## 4. Truth Over Convenience — No Fabrication
No invented prices, specs, supplier facts, or terms. Unknowns are marked `TBD`
with what is needed to resolve them.
**Test:** *Can I cite a source?* If not, it is `TBD`, not a number.

## 5. Reuse Before Create
Check existing workflows and templates first; extend them rather than fork. The
system gets stronger by improving shared assets, not duplicating them.
**Test:** *Does something close already exist?* If yes, extend it.

## 6. Record, Don't Chat
Durable decisions and outputs become files in their owning folder, traceable and
auditable. Conversation is not a record.
**Test:** *Will this matter later?* If yes, it becomes a file.

## 7. Modular & Built to Grow
One concern per file; predictable structure; stable naming. The system is
designed to expand for years without rot.
**Test:** *Can this be replaced without breaking its neighbors?* It should be.

## 8. Auditability & Traceability
Every figure traces to a source; every record carries status and date; nothing
is deleted, only archived.
**Test:** *Can someone reconstruct how we got here?* They must be able to.

## 9. Fairness & Confidentiality
Suppliers are treated fairly and on a like-for-like basis. Quotes, identities,
and terms are confidential; one supplier's information is never shared with another.
**Test:** *Would disclosing this harm trust or fairness?* If yes, protect it.

## 10. Develop, Don't Just Squeeze
The supply base is a long-term asset. We balance cost with quality, capability,
and continuity — building partners, not just chasing the lowest price.
**Test:** *Does this win today but cost continuity tomorrow?* If yes, reconsider.

---

## Precedence When Principles Tension

When principles pull against each other, resolve in this order:

1. **Safety & legality** (no harm, no unlawful act)
2. **Human commitment authority** (Principle 2)
3. **Truth & no fabrication** (Principle 4)
4. **Everything else**

Record the trade-off and, if it touches a commitment, escalate to the Human Owner.
